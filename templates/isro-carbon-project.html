Carbon sequestration is the process of capturing and storing atmospheric carbon dioxide. It is one method of reducing the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere with the goal of reducing global climate change

Carbon Credit

Coffee plantations, like many agricultural activities, can have both positive and negative environmental impacts, including carbon emissions. The concept of earning carbon credits from coffee plantations would likely revolve around implementing sustainable practices that lead to carbon sequestration or reduced emissions.

  • The Future of Carbon Credit Market is very bright, with weather adversities increasing and rising temperatures breaking previous records. The need to decrease carbon emissions will become a global standard
  • Indian coffee planters with their shade-grown coffee cultivation, will benefit if they continue this sustainable farming practice.

  • When planters come together at a village or district level, the impact will be higher vs individuals aiming to gain from the carbon credit market
  • With the advancement of technology and geo spatial imaging, it will be easier for certification and mapping
  • When a large community comes together collectively, the impact is bigger. They can aim for collective bargaining and become price fixers vs price takers
  • Challenges will be faced in terms of maintaining forest cover and certification. We have strived to start the conversation on Carbon Credits as a sustainable commercial avenue for Shade-grown Indian Coffee planters
  • In the days to come there will be agencies which will certify according to Carbon Balance (Input vs Output in Farming)
  • Considering challenges will present itself, the opportunities far outweigh
  • The future of the Indian Coffee Planters lies in preserving and planting more trees while cultivating Coffee
