Shade-Grown Indian Coffee Plantations & Carbon Sequestration

  • Indian Coffee plantations exists since 18th Century, thanks to early success of British Entrepreneurs
  • Indian coffee is identified as coffee grown under shade, on the world coffee map.
  • It’s primarily grown in the ecologically sensitive regions of the Western and Eastern Ghats. Among the 25 biodiversity hotspots of the world www.coffeeboard.gov.in
  • Data suggests, Indian Coffee Plantations have been around for 300+ years. Besides providing the world it’s exotic daily cup, the amount of carbon Indian coffee plantations has sequestered over centuries is immeasurable
  • The Green Giants (Trees) in the plantations have stood the test of time, provided livelihoods and sequestered carbon for centuries
  • Indian Coffee Planters are among the few farmers in the world who have this forest heritage cultivation dating back to 18th century
  • Our endeavor is to educate Indian Coffee Planters on:
    • Carbon sequestration in Indian coffee plantations
    • Estimated carbon sequestered in tons/acre annually
    • Commercial opportunities for coffee planters in carbon trading
    • Government of India’s Carbon Credit Trading Scheme, 2023
    • Future & Way Forward

    • Commercial Opportunities for Indian Coffee Planters in Carbon Credit Market

    • 1 carbon credit current pricing for Agro-forestry is $ 9-11 www.8billiontrees.com
    • 1 unit of carbon credit corresponds to 1 metric ton of carbon dioxide prevented from entering the atmosphere https://submer.com
    • “Studies estimate 1 Hectare of shade-grown coffee farm with large forest trees can sequester 70-80 tonnes of carbon. Which is more or less equivalent to the carbon stored in an equal area of a forest
    • On the contrary a full sun-grown or openly cultivated coffee farm in one hectare can only store less than 10 tonnes of carbon" or 4 tonnes per acre. Open cultivation is the way coffee is cultivated across the world https://www.researchgate.net/publication
    • Extrapolating further, 1 Acre of shade-grown coffee farm with large forest trees can sequester 28-32 Tonnes of Carbon annually.
    • Considering studies & current carbon credit pricing. Commercial Potential per Acre for shade-grown Indian Coffee Plantations with large forest trees is $ 280-320, translates to INR 22960-26640 annually.
    • With rapid increa in global warming and the need to reduce green-house gases. Carbon Credits will only be a growing market and it’s in our best interests to preserve shade trees in coffee plantations.
    • Having come this far for centuries cultivating coffee under shade, the future of Indian coffee planters lies in preserving and planting more trees while cultivating coffee and not in open cultivation which is the major practice across the world.
